Murray Has Surgery

(April 3, 2002) -- Raptors forward Tracy Murray underwent arthroscopic surgery on his right hip Tuesday afternoon in Pittsburgh, Pennsylvania, the Raptors announced. Dr. Mark Philippon performed the procedure at UPMC St. Margaret Hospital. 

Murray suffered from back spasms due to a long-term arthritic problem in his hip. He was placed on the injured list February 24. 

Murray will be sidelined for the remainder of the regular season. He is scheduled to return to Toronto on Thursday to begin rehabilitation. 

Murray averaged 5.7 points, 1.3 rebounds and 11.8 minutes in 40 games during the 2001-02 season.
