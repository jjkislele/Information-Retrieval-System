Raptors Sign Nate Huffman


(July 26, 2002) --- The Toronto Raptors announced Friday they have signed free agent centre Nate Huffman. Per team policy, terms of the deal were not disclosed. 

We are very happy to add Nate to our team,?said Glen Grunwald, senior vice-president and general manager. “With his success in Europe, Nate has shown that he is an excellent player and should have a significant impact on the team next season.? 

Huffman, 27, was a member of the three-time Israeli League champion and Israeli Cup champion Maccabi Elite Tel-Aviv. The 7-0, 242 pounder averaged 18.8 points, 7.5 rebounds and 27.6 minutes in 26 games with Maccabi Elite Tel-Aviv last season. Huffman was the MVP of both the Suproleague and Israeli League for the 2000-01 season and was also named the Best American Player in Europe for 2001 by Besket News. 

Huffman played the 1998-99 season with Fuenlabrada of the Spanish League, averaging 12.7 points, 8.6 rebounds and 27.9 minutes in 34 games. A member of the Idaho Stampede of the Continental Basketball Association for the 1997-98 season, he averaged 11.2 points, 7.6 rebounds and 25.1 minutes in 56 games. 

A native of Battle Creek, Michigan, Huffman played two years at Central Michigan where he averaged 15.8 points, 9.4 rebounds and 28.8 minutes in 45 games. He played his freshman and sophomore seasons at Lansing Community College.
